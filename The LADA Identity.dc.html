Chapter One 01 / 14

The Heart of LADA

Why LADA Exists
LADA was never created simply to become another reproductive medicine centre.
It was created because one question gradually became impossible to ignore.
Are we measuring the right thing?
For decades reproductive medicine has measured success through medical milestones.
Consultation.
Diagnosis.
Embryo.
Pregnancy.
Birth.
These milestones matter.
They always will.
But they are not the whole journey.
Technology has transformed reproductive medicine.
Science continues to advance.
Laboratories become more precise.
Treatments become more successful.
Yet one essential dimension often remains underdeveloped:
The preparation of the people who will become parents.
LADA believes the next evolution of reproductive medicine will not come from technology alone.
It will come from preparing human beings.
Emotionally.
Psychologically.
Educationally.
Responsibly.
Before life begins.

In Her Own Words Svitlana Yashchenok

The Journey

For years, I believed success could be measured by a pregnancy. Then I realised something much deeper.

It was the number we reported, the moment we celebrated, the outcome we had all been trained to pursue. And then, slowly, across thousands of families, I began to see something the numbers never showed.

The people who came to us were not only trying to conceive. They were being changed by the journey — by fear, by hope, by waiting, by learning. Some left us with a child. Some did not. But those who left stronger, calmer and more prepared had all been cared for as whole people, not only as patients.

That was the realisation I could not unsee. Medicine had given me the tools to treat a body. It had not yet taught me to prepare a life.

LADA is what I built from that understanding — a place where preparation matters as much as treatment, and where every family is helped to begin, whatever the outcome, a little more ready than before.

Svitlana Yashchenok · Founder, LADA
