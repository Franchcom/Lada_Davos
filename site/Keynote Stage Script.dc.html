Part One

The Stage Script

Read as one continuous thought, not as a list. The slides confirm what has just been said; they never lead it. Let each pause be real — silence is part of the argument.

Slide 01 · Opening ≈ 1:10

Before Life Begins

On screen
Title, portrait of the speaker, and the two figures — 18+ years, 3,500 families. Let the room settle on it before the first word.

I believe the greatest mistake we have made in reproductive medicine is that we measure success by medical milestones.

Pause — 2 to 3 seconds · no filler

My name is Svitlana Yashchenok. Almost twenty years ago, I founded the LADA Reproductive Medicine Center, in Odesa, Ukraine.

In that time, I have worked with people who came to us wanting, more than anything, to become parents. Some succeeded quickly. Many did not.

And somewhere along the way, I started to notice something that none of our medical metrics were capturing.

That is what I would like to talk about today — why I believe the future of reproductive medicine begins long before life itself begins.

Slide 02 · The Question ≈ 0:50

Milestones Aren't the Journey

On screen
The milestone timeline appears: Consultation · Diagnosis · Embryo · Pregnancy · Birth. Speak the list at the pace the eye reads it.

For as long as I can remember, our profession has judged itself by a sequence of milestones.

A consultation. A diagnosis. An embryo. A pregnancy. A birth.

Each one matters, of course. But none of them, on its own, tells you what actually happened to the person going through it.

Pause — let the timeline hang

They are moments. Not the journey itself.

Slide 03 · The Insight ≈ 0:35

Transformation of People

On screen
Full-bleed photograph — an expectant mother — with the line "We were measuring the wrong thing." This slide carries emotion; let the image speak before you do.
Pause — 3 seconds on the image

The journey is something else.

It is the transformation a person goes through while they are moving through all of this.

Once I understood that, I could not see my own work the same way again.

Slide 04 · The Shift ≈ 1:20

The LADA Difference

On screen
The five pillars, and the figure panel: 20% → 55%. Slow slightly on "the LADA difference"; save your emphasis for the data.

Over the past two decades, the science has moved forward enormously. Better laboratories. Better genetics. Far better technology than when I started.

But I do not think the next real breakthrough is going to come from another piece of equipment. I think it is going to come from how we prepare people — not just their bodies, but their whole selves — for parenthood.

This is what I think of as the LADA difference: combining the science with education, with prevention, with attention to emotional wellbeing, and with real, conscious preparation.

A healthy embryo is only part of the outcome we should be aiming for. A healthy, prepared future parent is just as important.

Pause — then lean into the data

And this is not just something I believe — there is data behind it. In published research, adding psychological support to standard IVF raised the pregnancy rate from 20 percent to 55 percent.

Same patients. Same medicine. The only difference was whether the whole person was cared for.

Slide 05 · The Model, Part One ≈ 0:55

Helplessness → Responsibility · Fear → Understanding

On screen
The first two transformations appear as the words are spoken. This is the core of the talk — think it through in real time, not by recitation.

This is really what led us to build what we now call the LADA Human Transformation Model.

Every person who walks into our clinic is, in some sense, at the beginning of a journey — and we have come to recognize the same shifts happening again and again.

The first is a shift from helplessness to responsibility. People start to understand that their reproductive future is not simply something that happens to them. It is something they can actively shape.

The second is a shift from fear to understanding. As people learn more, the uncertainty they came in with starts to give way.

Slide 06 · The Model, Part Two ≈ 1:00

Isolation → Partnership · Wanting a Baby → Becoming Parents

On screen
The final two transformations, resolving to "Stronger. Wiser. Hopeful." Let the last sentence slow of its own accord — do not push emotion into it.

The third shift is from isolation to partnership. Patients stop carrying this alone — they become part of a real partnership, with each other and with our team.

And the fourth is a shift from wanting a baby to becoming a parent. Because parenthood, in my experience, does not begin at birth. It begins with preparation, well before that.

Pause — before the closing line

And whatever the medical outcome turns out to be, what I hope for every person who comes through our doors is that they leave a little stronger, a little wiser, more resilient, and more hopeful than when they arrived.

Slide 07 · Redefining Success ≈ 0:55

Success, Redefined

On screen
The broader measures build down the slide — Children, Families, Parents, Decisions — resolving to Generations. Thoughtful, not triumphant.

So if you asked me today what success in reproductive medicine actually means, I would give you a different answer than I would have years ago.

It is not only about pregnancy rates. It is about healthier children. Healthier families.

Parents who are genuinely better informed, and able to make more conscious decisions.

Pause — let "healthier generations" land quietly

And, ultimately, it is about healthier generations — because a healthier family contributes, in a very real way, to a healthier society.

Slide 08 · Our Vision · Close ≈ 1:05

One Ecosystem

On screen
"One Ecosystem." dominant, resolving to "Healthier Generations" and "Thank you." This is the moment for the partners and investors in the room.

None of this is something a single clinic can achieve alone.

What I am working toward is one ecosystem — medical centres, education, certification, research, and government partnerships, all built around the same outcome: healthier generations.

I believe the future of reproductive medicine will not be defined only by new technology. It will be defined by how well we prepare the people who will create the next generation.

That is the future LADA wants to help build. And we are looking for partners who want to build it with us.

Pause — hold before the last two words

Thank you.

Slide 09 · Stay Connected Not spoken

Let's Continue the Conversation

On screen
Contact details and the QR code. This slide stays up through the applause and Q&A; it is not narrated.

Optional — only if the moment calls for it:

If anything here resonated with you, I would genuinely like to continue the conversation. My details are on the screen — please don't hesitate to reach out.

Part Two

The Presenter Version

For the speaker's eyes only. Timing, gaze, gesture and the moment to advance — everything needed to deliver the keynote naturally, and nothing the audience should ever see.

RegisterCalm and even throughout. No build-up, no rallying. Bigger, never louder.
RhythmAdvance only after a line has landed — the slide follows you.
TotalApproximately 7 to 8 minutes, plus silence. Do not hurry the pauses.

For Svitlana — before you walk on stage

The Speaker's Mindset

Do not try to impress.
Do not try to convince.
Do not try to sell.
Tell the story that only you can tell.
Speak slowly. Trust silence.
The audience has already chosen to listen.
Your task is not to give answers.
Your task is to invite people to think differently.
The slides support you.
The ideas belong to you.
