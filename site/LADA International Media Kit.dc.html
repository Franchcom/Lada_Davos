About LADA 03 / 12

A centre built around the person, not only the procedure.

For almost twenty years, the LADA Reproductive Medicine Center has accompanied families through one of the most demanding experiences in adult life. Its vision is integrated: uniting science, prevention, education and emotional wellbeing as one continuous form of care.

History

Founded in 2008 in Odesa, Ukraine, LADA has grown from a single clinic into a founder-led centre of international standing, having accompanied more than 3,500 families.

Mission

To care for the whole person — preparing individuals and families for parenthood, not only treating the condition that brought them to us.

Vision

A reproductive medicine measured not only by the pregnancies it achieves, but by the healthier generations it helps prepare.

Values

Human-centred medicine. Evidence over promise. Preparation as treatment. Dignity through every outcome.

The Human Transformation Model

Care that begins before treatment and continues beyond it — preparing the whole person as clinical work in its own right.

One Ecosystem

Medicine, research, education and prevention connected into a single continuum of care for healthier generations.

About the Founder 04 / 12
Svitlana Yashchenok
Founder, LADA Reproductive Medicine Center · Odesa, Ukraine

Eighteen years spent learning that medicine begins with the person.

Svitlana Yashchenok founded LADA in 2008 and has since led it through the care of more than 3,500 families. Her work sits at the meeting point of clinical reproductive medicine and the human experience of it — and it is from that vantage point that the LADA Vision has taken shape.

A founder rather than an administrator, she speaks internationally on the future of the field: on prevention, on the psychology of treatment, and on why the measure of success in reproductive medicine must widen beyond the pregnancy itself.
