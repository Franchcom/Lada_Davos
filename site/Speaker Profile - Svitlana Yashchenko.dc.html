Official Biography 02 / 10

Rethinking what success in reproductive medicine truly means.

Svitlana Yashchenok is the Founder of LADA Reproductive Medicine Center and has dedicated more than eighteen years to advancing reproductive healthcare. Throughout her career, she has supported more than 3,500 families, witnessing both the remarkable progress of modern fertility medicine and the limitations of measuring success solely through clinical outcomes.

These experiences inspired the development of a broader vision—one that views reproductive medicine not only as a medical discipline, but as an opportunity to prepare future parents emotionally, psychologically and educationally before pregnancy begins.

Today, Svitlana advocates for an integrated model that combines science, education, prevention and human transformation. Her work explores how reproductive medicine can contribute not only to successful pregnancies, but to healthier families, stronger communities and future generations.

Her keynote, "Before Life Begins," invites healthcare leaders, governments and international partners to rethink what success in reproductive medicine truly means.

Extended Biography 03 / 10

For more than eighteen years, Svitlana Yashchenok has worked at the intersection of reproductive medicine, clinical leadership and human care. As Founder of LADA Reproductive Medicine Center, she has accompanied more than 3,500 families through one of the most significant journeys of their lives—the path toward parenthood.

Over time, this experience led her to question an assumption that has shaped reproductive medicine for decades: is pregnancy alone the right measure of success?

While scientific and technological advances have transformed fertility treatment, Svitlana believes the next evolution of reproductive medicine will be defined not only by better technology, but by better preparation.

Her work proposes a broader framework that integrates medical excellence with education, emotional wellbeing, prevention and conscious preparation for parenthood. Rather than viewing reproductive medicine as a series of isolated clinical procedures, she advocates for a connected ecosystem that supports individuals before, during and beyond treatment.

This philosophy forms the foundation of the LADA Human Transformation Model, an approach that encourages a shift from fear to understanding, from uncertainty to responsibility, and from seeking pregnancy to preparing for lifelong parenthood.

Svitlana's vision extends beyond the walls of a medical centre. She believes that governments, healthcare systems, universities, researchers and strategic partners all have a role to play in creating healthier generations before life begins.

Her keynote "Before Life Begins" reflects this vision. It is an invitation to rethink reproductive medicine not as the treatment of infertility, but as a long-term investment in human health, family resilience and society.
